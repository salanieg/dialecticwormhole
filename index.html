Launchpad

Entropy shifts order into chaos. Not because it is necessary, however, but because it is the most probable outcome. The pure destruction of geometry, whether physical or discursive, is a masculine act. To arrange what has been destroyed, to synthesise it, is the legacy of Gaía. To force the shattered geometry through a dialectical wormhole, and to reassemble it, that is the desideratum of this website.

This essay explores the limitations of human independence in relation to their instincts and cultural conditioning. Muench argues that there is a blurred line between humans as "predators" and as "pets", both of which are subject to the same principles of dependency. Nietzsche's concept of the "instruments of culture" is used to highlight how humans have become dependent on institutionalised social structures, creating a new form of independence that is, in reality, a façade for their primal instincts.



This essay dscusses the role of imitation in mass culture, with a focus on the impact on individuality and creativity. The author argues that while the ability to imitate is important in creating cultural artifacts that appeal to the masses, the emphasis on imitation in mass culture has led to a loss of originality and a focus on pleasing the largest possible audience. Ultimately, the essay questions whether the imitation-driven culture of mass culture is stifling creativity or fostering collective creation.
